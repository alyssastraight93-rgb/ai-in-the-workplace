# Municipal AI Use Quick Reference Guide

## Project Overview
This repository contains a high-impact, one-page Quick Reference Guide designed to onboard municipal employees to the safe, effective, and accountable use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in local government workflows. 

The document translates complex technical compliance standards into an accessible, visually structured graphic—maximizing clarity for users ranging from technical administrators to front-line public service staff.

---

## Key Design Features & Methodology
* **Canva Production & Implementation:**
  Developed entirely within Canva to leverage precise grid alignments, modern typography, and clean asset framing, ensuring the document was highly readable and ready for enterprise-wide digital rollout or physical print.
* **Intentional Color-Coded Architecture:** 
  Designed with an intentional "When to Use" (Green) vs. "When NOT to Use" (Red) visual block structure. This specific layout was a requested design parameter from leadership to establish an immediate, unambiguous psychological boundary between secure practices and high-risk compliance hazards.
* **"Should I Use AI?" Four-Point Checklist:** 
  Implements a simple, actionable decision framework to enforce immediate accountability regarding personal data and quality checks before an employee launches a tool.
* **Practical Workplace Scenario ("Sarah vs. Tom"):** 
  Demonstrates a relatable behavioral contrast showing how AI should be used as an analytical assistant to get started, rather than a blind replacement for human oversight and knowledge.
* **Governance & Tenant Integrity:** 
  Explicitly details compliance boundaries regarding approved, secure enterprise environments (such as Copilot Chat integrated within a managed municipal Microsoft tenant).

## Real-World Impact & Industry Validation
The strength, clarity, and visual appeal of this layout resulted in immediate real-world validation within the public sector ecosystem. 

### Project Timeline & Adoption Velocity
* **Internal Launch (July 2, 2026):** The finalized guide was formally launched and distributed via email exclusively to internal municipal employees as an official IT resource.
* **External Adoption (July 8, 2026):** Within six business days of the internal release, the exact structural framework, layout style, and scenario formulas were adopted and implemented by the Town of North Haven, CT. This rapid adoption window serves as strong industry validation of the asset's utility and design quality.
